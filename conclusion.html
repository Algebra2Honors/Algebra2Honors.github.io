Conclusion

World Temperature:

For Problem A, the two models that I chose were cubic and quintic. Using their equations I ended up with the prediction that in 2030, the temperature will be 14.96°C (with the cubic model) or 18.26°C (with the quintic model). By reformatting the data to show the percent change from the previous year, I was surprised to learn that the world temperature is not continuously rising. The temperature is actually sometimes less than the year before. Viewing real data about something that affects my life was very interesting.

Electric Cars:

Initially, we planned on focusing the report on self-driving automobiles. We wanted to predict when 51% of automobile sales in the United States would be autonomous. We decided not to go through with this idea because there was no solid data for this topic. Currently, there are no fully autonomous vehicles for sale, so all the data we found was pure speculation. Because of this, we looked at electric vehicles as a potential topic. Electric vehicles are a large topic of discussion in the automotive world, and we were able to find solid data, so we focused on electric vehicles. However, we still wanted to incorporate autonomous vehicles into our project, so we decided to look at Tesla sales. Tesla is a leader in the automobile market when it comes to electric vehicles and driver assistance features, so we saw it as a great automobile manufacturer to bridge the worlds of electric vehicles and autonomous vehicles. We were able to find data on Tesla sales starting from the year 2011, and used that data to form our prediction for when 51% of annual vehicle sales in the United States would be Teslas. While we did not accomplish what we initially planned, we adapted and formed a project that we are very proud of. 

Clean Charging:

We knew that we wanted to do a second, very different report that still relates to our electric car report. Our first idea was to find when a new revolutionary transportation technology would become mainstream, based on how often humans switch modes of transportation. We gathered data about when humans changed from horses, to trains, to cars, to planes, but found that we were only able to get a few data points, and it did not seem like a prediction would be accurate. Our hesitation was confirmed when we realized that humans started using horses around 3500 BCE, which would greatly skew our data. Because of these difficulties, we switched to when electric charging stations will outnumber gas stations. The data for this topic soon proved to be surprisingly difficult to find, and after much searching we only came across a few data points. Therefore, we switched for the third time to ‘When will most of the electricity used for electric car charging come from clean sources?’. This was an interesting topic because before, I had not thought about whether our not the electricity itself was made using environmentally friendly techniques. I finally arrived at an answer: Not until late 2223 will most of our energy used to charge cars come from clean sources. Another aspect that I could have looked into would have been whether clean sources are being used disproportionately to charge cars, and therefore looking at the world’s power sources is not an accurate representation. However, this report was still intriguing to think about and the result was very surprising!
